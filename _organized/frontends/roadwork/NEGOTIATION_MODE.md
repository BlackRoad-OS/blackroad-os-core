**What it does:**
- Parse offer details (base, bonus, equity, benefits)
- Compare to market data (levels.fyi, Glassdoor, H1B data)

- Identify weak spots (low base, weak equity, missing benefits)
- Generate leverage score (competing offers, urgency, role criticality)

## 🎓 BUILT-IN NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORKS

### The Anchoring Framework
```
1. Let them anchor first (wait for their offer)
2. Express enthusiasm + pivot to concerns
3. Anchor your counter with market data
4. Justify with competing offer or unique value
5. Give them space to respond
```

### The Silence Framework
```
After you counter, STOP TALKING.
The first person to speak loses.
Wait for their response—don't fill the silence.
```

### The Multiple-Requests Framework
```
Ask for 3 things:
1. Primary (what you really want)
2. Secondary (nice to have)
3. Tertiary (easy for them to give)

They'll likely give you 1.5-2 of the 3.
```

### The "Excited But..." Framework
```
"I'm really excited about this opportunity, but I have concerns about [X].
Can we find a way to address that?"

Keeps the vibe positive while still pushing.
```

## 🧪 SIMULATION MODE

**Practice negotiation conversations with 4 recruiter personas:**

### 1️⃣ The Hardball Recruiter
**Style:** "This is our final offer. Take it or leave it."
**Response:** Validate urgency, test with soft counter, prepare to walk.

### 2️⃣ The Friendly Recruiter
**Style:** "I really want to make this work. Let me see what I can do."
**Response:** Be specific about requests, give them ammunition to advocate internally.

### 3️⃣ The Data-Driven Recruiter
**Style:** "Our comp is benchmarked to market. Can you share your data?"
**Response:** Provide levels.fyi links, H1B data, competing offer details.

### 4️⃣ The Slow-Walker
**Style:** "I'll need to discuss with the hiring manager and get back to you."
**Response:** Set clear timeline expectations, maintain momentum.
